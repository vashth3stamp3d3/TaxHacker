# RC4022 General Information for GST/HST Registrants

Official source: https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/forms-publications/publications/rc4022/general-information-gst-hst-registrants.html

CRA guide version observed: RC4022(E) Rev. 25.

CRA caution: This guide uses plain language to explain the most common tax situations. It does not replace the law. Unless otherwise stated, legislative references are to the Excise Tax Act or GST/HST Regulations.

## Scope

RC4022 provides basic GST/HST information for registrants, including:

- What the GST/HST is.
- Who pays GST/HST and who charges GST/HST.
- Taxable supplies, zero-rated supplies, and false GST/HST exemptions.
- Whether a person or business should register, including small supplier considerations.
- Voluntary registration.
- Reporting periods and reporting period changes.
- Making changes to a GST/HST account.
- Collecting GST/HST.
- Sales invoice requirements for GST/HST registrants.
- Input tax credit information requirements.
- Early-payment discounts, late-payment surcharges, and volume discounts.
- Input tax credits and ITC restrictions, including procurement cards and meals and entertainment.

## Alberta print shop context

Alberta has GST at 5% and no provincial sales tax or HST. A registered Alberta print shop generally charges GST on taxable supplies unless a supply is zero-rated or exempt. It may claim input tax credits only when GST/HST was paid or payable on eligible business inputs and documentation requirements are met. Foreign supplier invoices are generally outside Canadian GST/HST unless Canadian GST/HST is charged or self-assessment rules apply.

## Advisor usage

Use RC4022 for GST registration, invoicing, GST collection, ITC, filing, remittance, and documentation questions. For corporation income tax/T2 questions, use T4012. For payroll deductions and taxable benefits, use T4001 and T4130.
